Batangas Lomi Recipe

Welcome to my Batangas Lomi Recipe!

Batangas lomi, The first time I had this(well not really but it's the most memorable experience i've had eating a batangas lomi), was when my college friends were craving to eat some batangas lomi, and sure enough we found one near their hometown, again. so once we finished our class on that day we all head on to the Batangas lomi Karinderya

When we all finally got together on the Karinderya(Some of us have motorcycles, and some had to commute), We all sat down and looked at the menu. The servings they offer for the Batangas lomi ranges from good for 1 person up to 5-6 person per bowl, and sure enough we ordered the one good for 5-6 persons, each, well except one of our friend, who ordered the one good for 2-3 persons. I also wanted a smaller serving as well for my first time, but I succumbed to the peer pressure so I also ordered the MEGA size.

Once the waitress brought our orders, I was very surprised by size of that thing! even the waitress was shocked, she said she expected alot of people were there to eat since let me remind you that the serving that we ordered they said were for 5-6 persons and her seeing this 7 starved broke college students each order a plate of such quantity, she just laughed and told us if we are serious about this and we can always request for a takeout if we didn't finish it.

It was a very pleasant memory and yes, we all did finish our bowls.

Anyways here is the recipe I found online for the Batangas Lomi:

Prep Time

20 Mins

Cook Time

40 Mins

Total Time

1 Hr

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 medium white onion sliced
  • 250 Grams pork belly (liempo) sliced
  • 1 Cup kikiam sliced
  • 150 Grams pork liver sliced into strips
  • 2 pieces pork bouillon cubes
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 1/2 tbsp Soy sauce more to serve
  • 500 Grams fresh lomi noodles rinsed
  • 6 tbsp Cassava Starch dissolved in 3 tbsp cold water
  • 2 large eggs beaten
  • Salt to taste
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • chicharon crushed, for topping
  • meatballs for topping
  • fried garlic for topping
  • green onions Chopped, for topping
  • calamansi to serve
  • siling labuyo (bird's eye chili) to serve
  • white onions chopped, to served

Instructions:

  1. Saut√©ing Aromatics:

    Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Saute onions until fragrant.

  2. Cooking Meat:

    Add pork, and cook until browned. Add kikiam and saute until light browned. Add liver and cook until dark browned. Set some aside for topping.

  3. Building the Broth:

    Add pork bouillon cubes and mix. Add water and soy sauce. Cook until simmering.

  4. Cooking the Noodles:

    Add lomi noodles and simmer for about 5 minutes or until tender.

  5. Thickening the Broth:

    Add dissolved cassava starch and simmer until thick and sticky, about 5 minutes.

  6. Finishing Touches:

    Add eggs and stir immediately. Simmer for 1 minute. Season with salt and pepper.

  7. Serving:

    Divide into bowls and serve with desired toppings. Serve with soy sauce, calamansi, red chilies, and onions on the side.

And that's it! enjoy your Batangas lomi!
